What rules can we have on Mars?

The civilization built there will look differently depending on why, how and what we choose to do there. In the popular marketing book we learn that it makes sense to start with Why.

  • Why do we want to go there? Is it to just be there? Are we as simple as just plankton that enters everywhere just because it can?
  • If we won‚Äôt fight over energy resources there - would it be necessary to have money? Time will be always scarce for us so maybe use directly time units instead?
  • Is our goal to build a better society? Or the main goal is to make Mars habitable? It surely is going to be the first goal but that is a ‚Äòwhat‚Äô, not ‚Äòwhy‚Äô.
  • What will be the currency on Mars? Once the first community becomes self-sufficient and capable of expanding, trade will begin. Would barter be possible?
  • How to sustain life during first hundreds (thousands?) of years when the period of changing atmosphere will take place. Can we make it being dependant all the time to a complex technology?
  • How to initiate biodiversity? Do we have to take all the seeds from Earth? Ecological chains and impacts are very intricate. Can we have them with a human as the only animal?
  • Are we going to spread quickly or we will be like a growing pangaea?
  • Can global, starlink, martian communication prevent tribalism if we separate each other a lot?
  • Are we going to ship goods from Mars? Ores? Is the connection between planets really going to be popular when it comes to travelling?

What are your concerns? What questions would you have regarding this?

My secondary questions is how is Mars, how green can it be, what it hides below the surface.

My main curiosity is evolving around what we are going to take there within us. How our genes, the facts of being Homo Sapiens will play out.
